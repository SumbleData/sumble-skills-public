# {customer_name} territory plan

Generated by the **sumble-territory-planning** skill. Self-contained,
**zero-dependency** app for reviewing how your accounts are split across reps —
and fixing it.

## Run

Any machine with Python 3.10+ — no `pip install`, no venv:

```bash
python3 app.py
```

Then open <http://localhost:8002>. Override the port via `python3 app.py 9002`
or `PORT=9002 python3 app.py`.

## Work it in this order

1. **Overview** — is each segment balanced? Each bar is one rep's book, measured
   as **total account score** (not account count). The **darker part of the bar
   is the score they have actually worked** — a long light bar is a book being
   carried, not covered. A dashed outline appears once you approve moves,
   showing where the book lands.
2. **Fix double-allocations first.** Two reps owning CRM records for the *same
   company* distorts every other number, so the planner leaves those accounts
   alone until you resolve them in your CRM.
3. **Moves** — accept or reject each proposal. Balance bars and the balance
   index recompute immediately. Any owner can be overridden by hand from the
   dropdown; that records as a `manual` change.
4. **Export** — `actions.csv` is the hand-off to your CRM.

**This app never writes to your CRM.** Decisions are saved to `territory.csv`
as you make them; the export is the only output.

## The five highlights

| Highlight | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Not being worked** | Owned, but the owner has had no meeting, call, or outbound email with them in the activity window. These are the reassignment candidates. |
| **Strong but idle** | Top-quartile score **for its own segment**, and not being worked. The most expensive kind of neglect. |
| **Wrong segment** | The account's size puts it in one segment; its owner sells another. |
| **Unallocated** | No active rep owns it: nobody, a queue, or someone who has left the company. |
| **Double-allocated** | Two or more reps own CRM records that resolve to the same Sumble organization. The owner shown is whoever has the most activity. |

## How balance is measured

Per segment, per rep: **total account score**. The balance index is the
coefficient of variation (population stdev ÷ mean) of those totals — scale-free,
so it reads the same for a 5-account team and a 5,000-account one.

- **≤ 0.15 balanced** · **≤ 0.35 uneven** · **above that imbalanced**

Customers are excluded by default: a rep is not overloaded because they own
renewals, and moving a customer breaks a live relationship. Change this in
`territory-plan.json` → `balance.include_categories`.

## How moves are proposed

Four phases, least disruptive first:

1. **Wrong segment** → move to the correct segment's lightest book
2. **Unallocated** → assign to the lightest book in its segment
3. **Whitespace** → same, for net-new accounts
4. **Rebalance** → only now are already-owned accounts moved, most overloaded →
   most underloaded, picking the account that most nearly halves the gap

**An account with activity is never proposed for a move.** A rep who has met,
called, or emailed a prospect has something the balance maths cannot see. Worked
out-of-segment accounts are flagged for you to judge by hand instead.

Rebalancing stops once the segment reaches a balance index below 0.10, or once
moves reach 15% of that segment's accounts — whichever comes first.

## Activity numbers

Counts are **between the owning rep and that account only** — not across the
team. Only counts and dates are stored: no subjects, no transcripts, no message
bodies. Inbound email is tracked and displayed but does not on its own mark an
account "worked" (that's the prospect doing the work).

## Files

| File | What it is |
|---|---|
| `territory-plan.json` | The config: segments, the boundary rule, the rep roster, activity window + sources, balance and move policy. Edit it by hand and re-run the pipeline to change behaviour. |
| `territory.csv` | One row per Sumble org — identity, score, segment, owner, every flag, activity counts, and the current proposal. Your decisions are saved here. |
| `actions.csv` | Written on export: one row per approved owner change (`from_owner` → `to_owner`, with the reason and the evidence). |
| `_raw/` | The inputs the plan was built from, plus `_territory_audit.json`. |

## Re-running

After editing `territory-plan.json`, or once new activity is available:

```bash
python3 _build/merge_territory.py --raw _raw     # rebuild the sheet
python3 _build/suggest_moves.py --dir .          # refresh proposals
```

Re-running **preserves your decisions**: accepted and manual moves are treated as
already applied, and rejected proposals are never suggested again. Pass
`--reset` to `suggest_moves.py` to start from scratch. Restart `app.py` to pick
up the new sheet.
